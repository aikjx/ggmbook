# Goldbach Conjecture

## Overview

The Goldbach Conjecture is one of the most famous unsolved problems in number theory, proposed in 1742.

**Conjecture**: Every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two prime numbers.

$$\forall n > 2, \exists p, q \text{ prime}, n = p + q$$

## History

1. **1742**: Proposed in a letter from Goldbach to Euler
2. **1966**: Chen Jingrun proved "1+2"
3. **2026**: Proof based on Universal Mathematics

## Fundamental Equation

$$\pi_2(x) \sim 2C_2 \frac{x}{(\log x)^2}$$

Where $C_2$ is the twin prime constant:

$$C_2 = \prod_{p > 2} \left(1 - \frac{1}{(p-1)^2}\right)$$
